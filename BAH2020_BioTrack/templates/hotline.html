Ways You Can Find Help Now

If you are in situation in which you need to find help now, there are many ways to find help based on the circumstances of the situation. In an effort to help you in any way possible, we have linked several phone numbers and websites in which you can get the help you need.

Call the Police

Call 911!

  • Call if you or someone close to you needs immediate assistance.
  • Despite recent controversy, there are effective police officers that are always available to help.
  • If necessary, click this button to call now!
Learn about how the police can help you
Disaster Destress Helpline

If you are suffering the loss of a close relative or struggling to relive a difficult moment, call or text this number now.

  • Call Now!
  • Text TalkWithUs for English or Hablanos for Spanish to 66746.
  • Spanish speakers from Puerto Rico can text Hablanos to 1-787-339-2663.
Learn about the Disaster Destress Hotline
National Suicide Prevention Lifeline

If you are contemplating harming yourself or even taking your life, it is best to talk to someone.

  • For English, call 1-800-273-8255.
  • For Spanish,call 1-888-628-9454.
  • To talk with people who may be experiencing a similar situation, check out the Lifeline Crisis Chat.
Learn about how the Suicide Lifeline can help you
National Domestic Violence Hotline

If you are a victim of domestic violence or know someone who is, it is important to make your voice heard.

  • Call 1-800-799-7233.
  • Text LOVEIS 22522.
  • Identify whether your situation is abuse or not.
Learn about how the National Domestic Violence Hotline can help you
National Child Abuse Hotline

If you are a victim of child abuse or know someone who is, it is important to notify the appropriate officials as soon as possible.

  • Call 1-800-422-4453.
  • You can also text this number as well.
Learn about how the Childhelp National Child Abuse Hotline can help you
SAMHSA's National Helpline

Find a health care provider or treatment for substance use disorder and mental health

  • Call 1-800-662-4357.
  • Teletype 1-800-487-4889.
Learn about how SAMHSA can help you.
