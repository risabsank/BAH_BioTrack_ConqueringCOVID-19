Learn More About the Virus That has Taken Over the World

Though the virus has been one of the most deadly viruses in recent history, people across the world seem to lack knowledge of the adverse health effects of the virus. Some overestimate the seriousness of the virus, causing constant paranoia, while others tend to undermine the seriousness of the virus. This page is dedicated to keeping you informed about the virus and how to take precautions to prevent the spread of the virus.

COVID-19 Cases

With COVID-19 cases rising up at an exponential rate, it is important to increase your knowledge on the virus to become more adept in fighting it. By learning more about the virus, you are also learning more about how to stop it from spreadng to your closed ones. As of July 15, there are 3,416,428 cases in the USA, 135,991 deaths as a result of the virus. Though the disease seems unstoppable, it is very preventable.


Common Symptoms of COVID-19:

  • Fever or chills
  • Cough
  • Shortness of breath or difficulty breathing
  • Fatigue
  • Muscle or body aches
  • Headache
  • New loss of taste or smell
  • Sore throat
  • Congestion or runny nose
  • Nausea or vomiting
  • Diarrhea

If you see someone with these symptoms, they should seek medical care immeidately:

  • Trouble breathing
  • Persistent pain or pressure in the chest
  • New confusion
  • Inability to wake or stay awake
  • Bluish lips or face

Precautions to Take to Mitigate Spread of Disease:

  • Clean your hands often, either with soap and water for 20 seconds or a hand sanitizer that contains at least 60% alcohol.
  • Avoid close contact with people who are sick.
  • Cover your mouth and nose with a cloth face cover when around others.
  • Cover your cough or sneeze with a tissue, then throw the tissue in the trash.
  • Clean and disinfect frequently touched objects and surfaces daily.
  • Cloth face coverings may help prevent people who have COVID-19 from spreading the virus to others.
